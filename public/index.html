About Us

Well Wishes sole goal is to serve underserved people with illnesses in developing countries world-wide. We are a donation-based medical care package service for an at-risk or terminally ill person in third-world countries. A Community Representative or Care Facility will login to the site and submit requests for an individual who has medical needs. We create a list of Wishes from the submitted request for Well Wish Donors to purchase. The purchased Wishes are sent to the requestor (Community Representative/ Care Facility) for delivery to the person in need.

We currently service Nigeria, Ghana, and Haiti. More Wishes, and services will be added as we expand our network of communities and countries

Nigeria

Noncommunicable diseases (NCDs) will likely be the most common cause of death in Africa by 2030. Noncommunicable diseases can both be contained by increasing resources for prevention, screening, early treatment, and regular monitoring.Health improvement strategies require materials for patient self-monitoring. These materials include blood pressure and glucose monitoring and medications as well as medical and first aid supplies.

Haiti

The top causes of death in Haiti are Cardiovascular Disease and Diarrhea (from Cholera). Cholera is a water-borne illness that is spread through contaminated water or food. Haiti is susceptible to hurricanes and is also at risk for earthquakes. Normal rains can cause floods in urban areas because of the severe deforestation throughout the island.Health improvement strategies require materials for patient self-monitoring for noncommunicable diseases and Cholera. These materials include blood pressure and glucose monitoring and medications as well as isotonics, medical supplies, and first aid supplies

Ghana

The top causes of death in Ghana are Lower Respiratory Infections(LRI), Stroke, and Malaria. Malaria, transferred by the Anopheles mosquito, is one of the most severe illnesses in the country, both by infection and mortality rate.The Ghanaian Ministry of Health is trying to make antimalarial drugs more affordable. Access to antimalarial drugs has increased over the last ten years. Health improvement strategies require materials for patient self-monitoring for noncommunicable diseases and Malaria. These materials include blood pressure and glucose monitoring and medications as well as antimalarial medications, medical and first aid supplies.
